Business Statement

Hon GERRY BROWNLEE (Leader of the House) : At the conclusion of business today, the House will adjourn for 1 week. When we resume on Tuesday, 18 August it is the Government’s intention to complete the estimates debate and make progress on the first readings of bills on the Order Paper. Wednesday, 19 August will be a members’ day.

Hon DARREN HUGHES (Senior Whip—Labour) : I wonder whether the Leader of the House could let us know when he expects the Immigration Bill to actually be brought to the floor of the House. He has mentioned it in several business statements and has signalled that it was the Government’s intention to progress that legislation, but it does not appear to have made its way here to the House.

Hon GERRY BROWNLEE (Leader of the House) : It is, of course, the Government’s intention to progress that bill. I did not mention it in today’s business statement, but the Government, as always, is open to cooperation from the Opposition to advance these matters. If the shadow Leader of the House wishes to approach me to accommodate such a move, then of course there will be discussions.
