10. Auckland Governance—Consultation

[Uncorrected transcript—subject to correction and further editing.]

10. PHIL TWYFORD (Labour) to the Minister of Local Government: Does he support the right of Aucklanders to decide on the future ownership of publicly owned assets under the super-city?

Hon RODNEY HIDE (Minister of Local Government) : Yes, I believe in local decisions being made at the local level, not only in Auckland but also right across New Zealand. The Local Government Act 2002 already requires councils to consult the public on any plans to buy or sell strategic assets.

Phil Twyford: Does the Minister stand by his proposal that councils undertake referenda on decisions that are significant or irreversible; if so, does he believe that the sale of publicly owned assets by the Auckland super-city would be significant or irreversible?

Hon RODNEY HIDE: I certainly stand by my statements. That is one of the issues that we have under review and are discussing with Local Government New Zealand, councils, and other stakeholders now.

Phil Twyford: Does the Minister stand by his statement on Campbell Live last night that the Prime Minister has ruled out the sale of Auckland’s publicly owned assets?

Hon RODNEY HIDE: Yes.
