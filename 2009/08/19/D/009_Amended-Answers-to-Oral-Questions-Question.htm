Amended Answers to Oral Questions — Question No. 3 to Minister

[Volume:656;Page:5712]

Amended Answers to Oral Questions

Question No. 3 to Minister

Hon TONY RYALL (Minister of Health) : I seek leave to make a personal statement to clarify an answer given in the House earlier today.

Mr DEPUTY SPEAKER: Leave is sought for that purpose. Is there any objection? There is no objection.

Hon TONY RYALL: Earlier this afternoon I told the House that I had not received a draft copy of the independent report of the ministerial review group. On reflection, I need to clarify that statement, as it was based on a literal interpretation of the question. It is correct that I did not get a draft of the full report. However, I did receive some early drafts of part of the report. A staff member also received a draft copy of annex 6, and I will table these at the end of my statement.

The material covered about 50 pages of what turned out to be a 190-page report. I received the ministerial review group final report on Friday, 31 August, the due date. Dr Horn advised me that he had discovered some typographical errors, and delivered a corrected copy the following Monday. Dr Horn advises me that no recommendations or content were changed. I am aware that a table of contents and biographical details were inserted over the weekend by Dr Horn. I maintained regular contact with Dr Horn and members of the ministerial review group, including having a dinner with them. As the Acting Prime Minister said today, the Minister of Health responsibly discussed with the review group what recommendations it was making. I want to make it clear that at no stage, as I said in the House today, did I issue any instructions that recommendations be changed, and I apologise to the House for any incorrect impression given by my answer earlier today.

Mr DEPUTY SPEAKER: Thank you.

Hon TONY RYALL: I seek leave to table four documents.

Mr DEPUTY SPEAKER: Leave is sought for that purpose. Is there any objection?

Hon Trevor Mallard: I raise a point of order, Mr Speaker. Just before that, can we have it clarified that it is the full draft report received by his office by a staff member on the Friday that he—

Mr DEPUTY SPEAKER: I ask the Minister to respond.

Hon TONY RYALL: No, it is not that. These are the drafts that I received.

Mr DEPUTY SPEAKER: They are the drafts that the Minister received. Leave is sought to table those documents. Is there any objection? There is no objection.
