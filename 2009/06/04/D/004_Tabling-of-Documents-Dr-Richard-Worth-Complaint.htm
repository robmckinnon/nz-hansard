Tabling of Documents — Dr Richard Worth—Complaint Regarding Conduct

[Volume:654;Page:4259]

Tabling of Documents

Dr Richard Worth—Complaint Regarding Conduct

Hon PHIL GOFF (Leader of the Opposition) : I apologise for interrupting my colleague. I seek leave of the House to table a statement from a complainant whose case I took to Mr Key. It sets out the nature of the concerns she has with respect to Dr Worth—

The ASSISTANT SPEAKER (Eric Roy): I think we know what the document is. Is anyone opposed to that course of action? There is.

Hon PHIL GOFF (Leader of the Opposition) : I raise a point of order, Mr Speaker. I have just come down to the Chamber from my office, where I heard the leader of the National Party, the Prime Minister, Mr Key, challenging me to present the evidence. I am doing just that, so perhaps the whip would reconsider his answer.

CHRIS TREMAIN (Junior Whip—National) : I am more than happy if the—

The ASSISTANT SPEAKER (Eric Roy): The member will seek leave again.

CHRIS TREMAIN: No, hold on. That is not what I am asking. If Phil Goff is happy to wait for 5 minutes, I will check as to whether that tabling is OK. Then I would ask him to seek leave again.

The ASSISTANT SPEAKER (Eric Roy): We are getting outside actual points of order here. Leave has been declined in an exchange that is probably not within the realms of order. I think a message has been passed.

Hon PHIL GOFF (Leader of the Opposition) : Mr Assistant Speaker Roy, I am happy for the Government whip to make that phone call.
