Urgency

Hon GERRY BROWNLEE (Leader of the House) : I move, That urgency be accorded to the introduction and first reading of the Electoral Amendment Bill and Government orders of the day Nos 1 and 2. The Electoral Amendment Bill deals with the controversial Electoral Finance Act, which caused a great deal of grief for a lot of New Zealanders prior to the last election. It was a commitment of the National Party, going into the election, to, in fact—

Hon Darren Hughes: Turn the page.

Hon GERRY BROWNLEE: —sorry—advance that bill. It is our intention today to make very significant progress with the bill, which will be read a first and second time this evening.

A party vote was called for on the question, That urgency be accorded.
Ayes 69 New Zealand National 58; ACT New Zealand 5; Māori Party 5; United Future 1.
Noes 53 New Zealand Labour 43; Green Party 9; Progressive 1.
Motion agreed to.
